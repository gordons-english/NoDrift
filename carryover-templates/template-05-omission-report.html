Template 05

Omission Report

Use this at the end of a capsule to prevent important operative detail from being quietly compressed, skipped, or hidden behind vague instructions.

User Instructions

Fill this report after every serious capsule. The goal is to make omissions visible. If no known important operative detail was intentionally omitted, state that directly. If anything was omitted, state what, why, the risk, and whether it must be recovered before the new chat acts.

Do not use this report as permission to omit important context. It is a safety audit. If a missing item is high risk, the correct result may be BLOCKED until the source is recovered or the user approves proceeding with limitations.

Use this when

  • A capsule is intended to be pasted into a new chat.
  • The source history is long, compacted, or partly unavailable.
  • Important details may be too long to fit comfortably in one response.

Do not use this when

  • You plan to hide omissions for brevity.
  • The missing detail is required for safety and should block the handoff.

End Directive And Readiness Questions

DIRECTIVE TO THE NEW CHAT

Before relying on the capsule, answer these questions from the omission report. Do not proceed as if missing high-risk information is harmless.

Governance understanding questions:
1. Were any important operative details intentionally omitted?
2. Why were they omitted?
3. What risk level was assigned to each omission?
4. Which omissions must be recovered before action?
5. What recovery path is available?
6. Does any omission require stopping?
7. Was anything omitted merely for brevity?
8. Are any omissions unknown because the source is unavailable?
9. What does the omission risk conclusion permit?
10. What does the omission risk conclusion prohibit?

Source-material understanding questions:
1. What project facts are incomplete?
2. What approved wording is missing?
3. What decisions are missing?
4. What files or artifacts are missing?
5. What QA or verification status is missing?
6. What risks are missing?
7. What open questions are missing?
8. What external-action boundaries are missing?
9. Can the next safe action proceed without the omitted item?
10. What must be recovered before responsibility transfers?
